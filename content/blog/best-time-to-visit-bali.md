# Best Time to Visit Bali: A Month-by-Month Guide

Bali is a year-round destination, but knowing when to visit can make a huge difference to your experience. Whether you want golden sunsets on Seminyak beach, spiritual ceremonies in Ubud, or adventure in the lush interior — timing is everything.

## The Dry Season (April – October)

This is Bali's **peak season** and the best time for most travellers. Expect:

- Clear skies and minimal rainfall
- Ideal conditions for beach activities and surfing
- The famous Galungan and Kuningan festivals (if they fall during your visit)
- Higher prices and busier attractions — book early!

**Best months:** June, July, and August offer the driest weather, but also the largest crowds. For a sweet spot, aim for **April–May** or **September–October**.

## The Wet Season (November – March)

The wet season doesn't mean constant rain. Showers are typically short and intense, leaving the rest of the day clear. Benefits include:

- Lush green landscapes — the rice terraces are stunning
- Fewer tourists and better hotel deals
- Great for surfing on the west coast (the swells are excellent)

**December and January** see the most rainfall, but Bali's temples and ceremonies are particularly magical during this period.

## Month-by-Month Quick Reference

| Month | Weather | Crowds | Best For |
|-------|---------|--------|----------|
| January | Wet | Low | Budget travel, photography |
| February | Wet | Low | Ubud arts & culture |
| March | Transition | Medium | Nyepi (Day of Silence) |
| April | Dry | Medium | Hiking, waterfalls |
| May | Dry | Medium | Relaxed beaches |
| June | Dry | High | Festivals, surf |
| July | Dry | Very High | Beach clubs, nightlife |
| August | Dry | Very High | All activities |
| September | Dry | Medium | Best overall balance |
| October | Transition | Low-Medium | Rice harvest season |
| November | Wet | Low | Budget, photography |
| December | Wet | High | Christmas & New Year |
